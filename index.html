About Me

I am a Graduate Research Assistant in Computer Science at the University of Michigan working with Kevin Fu. I graduated from Clemson University with a BS in Computer Engineering in 2015 and I am currently pursuing a PhD in Computer Science at the University of Michigan. I am interested in attacking systems below the common abstractions at the often forgotten physical layer and defending systems against such attacks. I speak both English and Japanese, and some of my interests include sports (especially football), climbing, and Japanese culture.

Research Experience

Clemson University

2015-2016

At Clemson, I primarily worked on ultra low power sensing network research with my undergraduate research advisor Jacob Sorber and then PhD student, now assistant professor, Josiah Hester. We produced Shoulder Angel, which can be found in my publications below.

University of Michigan

2016-Present

At Michigan, I focus on cyber-physical attacks and defenses. I am currently working on several projects. Feel free to send me an email for details.

SoK: A Minimalist Approach to Formalizing Analog Sensor Security

Systematizing knowledge of analog attacks and defenses against sensor circuitry via a novel framework that enables prediction of future attacks and defenses. The paper appeared in the IEEE Symposium on Security and Privacy (Oakland) in May 2020.
